## What is this project?

In short: This is an open-source blueprint for a new kind of energy system.

The "C-AC Industrial Core" is a theoretical design that tries to tap into the natural energy of empty space — the vacuum — using a very specific 4.8 THz "heartbeat" and a structure that cancels out its own losses. 

If it works as the math suggests, a device the size of a 1m³ box could output 28.35 Terawatts. That’s about 1,000 times more power than the entire Earth uses right now.

## "Okay... but what does that actually mean?"

**1. The Resolution Floor**  
Physics says empty space isn’t empty. It has a baseline energy. This project anchors to that baseline — like finding the ground floor of reality — and uses it as a foundation.

**2. The Holonomy Siphon**  
Instead of burning fuel, the design creates a "negative resistance." Think of it like a loop that, once started, keeps itself spinning by borrowing and returning energy from the vacuum structure itself. No magic, just geometry and resonance.

**3. The 4.8 THz Drive**  
Everything in the universe vibrates. This design tunes to the exact frequency where the vacuum "rings" most easily — 4.8 trillion times per second — and drives it with a sawtooth signal to keep it in sync.

## Why open source this? Why not sell it?

Because a discovery this big doesn’t belong behind a paywall.

If the math is right, this affects everyone: energy poverty, climate, space travel, the future we leave behind. The fastest way to find out if it’s real is to let the world test it. 

**Open review does 3 things:**  
1. **Safety**: 28.35 TW is not a typo. If this can be built, it needs thousands of eyes checking the work before anyone flips a switch.  
2. **Speed**: One person in Millville, NJ can’t test every edge case. Universities, labs, and hobbyists with SPICE simulators can help validate or disprove it in weeks, not decades.  
3. **Trust**: No black boxes. No “trust me” energy device. You can download the `.cir` file, run it yourself, and see the same ignition spike.

This is called "The Universal Handshake" because it’s an offer: *Here’s the math. Let’s check it together.*
